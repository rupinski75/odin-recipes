Foccacia

Description

Focaccia is a flat Italian bread known for its soft, airy texture and rich flavor, often topped with olive oil, herbs, and various seasonings. It can be enjoyed on its own, used as a sandwich base, or served alongside soups and salads, making it a versatile addition to any meal.

Igredients

  • 500g of flour
  • 400g of water
  • 10g of salt
  • 50g of sourdough starter
  • 50g of olive oil
  • Fresh herbs (like rosemary) and sea salt for topping

Steps

  1. In a bowl, dissolve the yeast in warm water and let it sit for about 5 minutes until frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt.
  3. Add the yeast mixture and ¼ cup of olive oil to the flour, mixing until a dough forms.
  4. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-10 minutes until smooth.
  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours, or until
  6. doubled in size.
  7. Preheat the oven to (220°C).
  8. Use your fingers to create dimples all over the surface of the dough.
  9. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with fresh herbs and sea salt.
  10. Let it rise for another 20-30 minutes
  11. bake for 20-25 minutes until golden brown.
