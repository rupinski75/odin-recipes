Boiling Water

Description

Boiling water occurs when it reaches a temperature of 100 degrees Celsius (212 degrees Fahrenheit) at sea level, causing it to transition from a liquid to a gaseous state as steam. This process is commonly used in cooking, sterilization, and various scientific applications, as boiling water can effectively kill bacteria and dissolve substances.

Igredients

  • Pot
  • Water
  • Stove
  • Oxygen

Steps

  1. Pour the desired amount of water into a pot
  2. Place the pot or kettle on the stove over high heat and cover it with a lid to speed up the boiling process.
  3. Once the water reaches a rolling boil (bubbles continuously rising to the surface), it is ready for use. Remove from heat as needed.
